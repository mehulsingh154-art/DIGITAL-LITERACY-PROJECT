Here is a simple checklist for responsible social media use as a college student:

Do’s:
1.Do verify before sharing
Check the source and accuracy of information before reposting or commenting to avoid spreading misinformation.

2.Do respect privacy
Ask permission before posting photos, stories, or details about others, especially in sensitive or private situations.

3.Do maintain a professional image
Keep language and content appropriate; remember that future employers, professors, and peers may see your profile.

4.Do set privacy and safety settings
Use account‑privacy options, block or report abusive users, and enable two‑factor authentication where possible.

5.Do balance screen time with real‑life engagement
Limit constant scrolling and dedicate time to in‑person study, exercise, and socializing.

Don’ts:
1.Don’t post hateful or harassing content
Avoid bullying, discrimination, or aggressive comments; these can harm others and damage your own reputation.

2.Don’t overshare personal information
Avoid posting home addresses, financial details, passwords, or overly sensitive personal data.

3.Don’t ignore online drama
Don’t engage in public feuds, screenshots meant to shame, or anonymous trolling; step away or report instead.

4.Don’t plagiarize or misuse content
Don’t copy others’ work, images, or code without credit; always acknowledge the original creator.

5.Don’t let social media replace reflection
Don’t compare your entire life to others’ highlight reels; remember that curated posts are not full reality.
