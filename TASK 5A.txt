Stay Safe Online – Checklist for College Students in India

Use this checklist to protect yourself online and on your UPI/financial apps:

Use strong, unique passwords
Create different passwords for each important account (email, bank, social media) and change them regularly. Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols, or use a trusted password‑manager app.

Enable two‑factor authentication (2FA)
Turn on 2FA on your email, social‑media, and banking/UPI apps so that even if someone gets your password, they cannot log in without the OTP or app code.

Check links and sender details carefully
Before clicking any link in an email, WhatsApp message, or Instagram DM, verify the sender’s name and email/phone number. If doubtful, open the official website or app directly instead of using the link.

Avoid public Wi‑Fi for sensitive tasks
Do not log in to your bank, UPI, or college‑portal accounts on public Wi‑Fi; use your mobile data or a trusted, password‑protected network.

Never share PINs, OTPs, or UPI PINs
No bank, merchant, or “support team” will ever ask you to share your OTP or UPI PIN. If anyone asks for it, end the call or block the contact and do not proceed.

Verify QR codes and payee details before UPI payments
Before scanning a QR code or sending money via UPI, confirm the receiver’s name, account number, or registered mobile number. Do not pay based on a screenshot or message from an unknown person.

Regularly check your bank and UPI transaction history
Enable SMS/WhatsApp alerts for all transactions and review your history weekly so you can spot and report any unauthorized debits immediately.

Report suspicious activity quickly
If you face an online scam, phishing attempt, or unauthorized UPI/bank transaction, report it at the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (https://cybercrime.gov.in) or call the National Cyber Crime Helpline 1930.
